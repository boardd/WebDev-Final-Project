History of Umami:

From the Chinese Character: 旨
4th Century
Meanings of 旨 in Chinese (/*jǐ/ Tang Dynasty): Excellent, usually used to describe food (食~) or alcohol (~酒)
From the Japanese Word: うま
17th - 18th Century
Meanings of 旨 in Japanese ( /*kijʔ/): (うま uma.i [ɯma]) delicious
To the English Word: Umami
1960s
Borrowed into English in the 1960s, specifically earliest evidence 1963 (2). First invented in 1909 by a Japanese Chemist (1)
