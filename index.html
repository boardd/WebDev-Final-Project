Phonology

When borrowed from Japanese, the only physical change that happened is the pronunciation of the word initial ‘u’, a change from /ɯ/ to /u/. /ɯ/ is a High-back vowel. It is close to the American /u/. However, it is pronounced with less protruded and less rounded lips than the American /u/.
